Daniel Defoe

"the father of the English novel"
(1660-1731)

His life

Early life

  • born into a middle-class family of Dissenters;
  • received education in Dissenting academy;
  • studied practical subjects (geography, modern languages and economy).

Adult life

  1. business:

    he went bankrupt twice as a merchant and got imprisoned for his debts;

  2. journalism:

    he wrote in Whig papers and published periodically The Review;

  3. intellectual

    he gained fame for his political essays and pamphlets.

In 1703, he got arrested for his writings under the reign of Queen Anne, who was against Nonconformists.
He is condemned to the pillory, but he is acclaimed by the people.
He then denies his Whig ideas for freedom and starts writing for the Tories.

Writing novels

Despite starting only at 59 years old, Defoe would become most famous for his novels. He managed to make live comfortably but died in 1731 while being heavily in debt.
